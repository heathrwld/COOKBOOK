Pork Nilaga Soup

Filipino Pork Nilaga Soup is a Boiled Pork Soup with Potato and Bok Choy. It is a simple way to prepare soup the Pinoy style. It goes with rice and is best with some spicy fish sauce on the side. Typically, the spicy fish sauce dip is simply fish sauce with chopped spicy chili. Siling labuyo is the hottest chili that originated from the Philippines. This is around 50,000 to 100,000 Scoville units.

instructions

step 1

Pour water in a cooking pot. Let boil.

step 2

Add onion and whole peppercorn. Continue to boil until the onion becomes tender.

step 3

Put the pork belly into the cooking pot. Let the water re-boil.

step 4

Add the pork cube. Stir. Cover the pot and continue to cook in low heat for 45 to 60 minutes or until the pork gets tender.

step 5

Add potato and saba banana. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes.

step 6

Add the fish sauce and pechay or bok choy. Stir. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.

step 7

Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.

step 8

Share and enjoy!

ingredients

2 Pounds Pork Belly

1 Pork Cube

3 to 4 Small Potato Peeled and Sliced in Half

2 Pieces Ripe Saba Banana, Sliced

6 to 8 Ounces Pechay or Bok Choy

1 Medium Yellow Onion, Sliced

Water

1 Tablespoon Whole Peppercorn

2 to 3 Tablespoons Fish Sauce
